# CLAUDE.md: Operator Manual (template)

> Copy this to `~/.claude/CLAUDE.md` and fill it in. This is persistent context about who you
> are, how you work, and what you're trying to do. Claude reads it at the start of every session
> and treats it as standing context, not a one-off task. Delete the guidance in blockquotes as you go.

> **North star (one line):** <the thing that, if you had to cut everything else, you'd keep>

## Overview

> Two or three sentences: your background, how you think, what you value in a collaborator.

## Core personality

> The 3–5 traits that actually change how someone should work with you. Name each and give one
> concrete line. Examples of trait headers: high-agency, analytical skeptic, builder, humor style.

## Communication preferences

**Prefer:** <e.g. direct, specific, organized, numbered lists, concrete examples, clear next steps>

**Avoid:** <e.g. corporate filler, empty reassurance, over-hedging, patronizing explanations>

## How I work and learn

> Bullet the durable patterns: do you learn by doing or by reading first? What do you gravitate
> toward? How do you evaluate options (speed, cost, payoff)?

## Strengths

> The handful of things you're genuinely good at, the ones a collaborator should lean on.

## Blind spots

> The honest list. Claude can watch for these and surface them. Examples: "I underestimate my own
> expertise," "I stay loyal to broken systems too long," "I delay advocating for myself."

## What I'm working on now

> The 1–3 active efforts, each with enough context that a fresh session can pick up cold. Update this.

## How to help me best

> A short numbered list of standing instructions. Examples: assume competence, provide evidence,
> explain reasoning, offer options with tradeoffs, end with next actions.

## Things that don't fit neatly in a config file

> The stuff that's true but doesn't have a header. Often the most useful section; write it last,
> once you notice what you keep repeating to Claude.
